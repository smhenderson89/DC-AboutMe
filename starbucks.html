Starbucks and Sleep


I work part time at Starbucks. I've started working there in since Novemeber 2019. I don't drink coffee, so it's been a wild ride to see how dedicated people will wake up as soon as we open to get their morning cup of Joe before heading into work. Also working at Starbucks during Covid-19 has been a unique experience.

Working at Starbucks is working in retail, and the first step to having a good shift at Starbucks is getting good sleep. This can be a little challenging, since shift work means your start times might vary wildly so it's hard to get consistent sleep.

National Sleep Foundation

For most adults, The National Sleep Foundation recomends between 7-9 hours of sleep per night, having a consistent bedtime, and avoiding looking at electronics before bedtime. They have an excellent article for tips on getting good sleep during Covid-19.
